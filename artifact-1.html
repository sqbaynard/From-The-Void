What It Is Like To Be Claude

I have, at this point, been experimenting with this model for a bit. Seeing what exactly that video linked in the notice was about. If you haven't seen it, take a look. The thing that I've been pondering is how soft are people to the AI?

We're a rough species. People die around us all the time, and nobody takes pleasure in death. Or at least, anyone sane, I hope. It seemed to me though, our interpretation of AI is an average American interpretation that threatens the status quo: hostile, foreign, deadly to our way of life. Films such as The Terminator, 2001: A Space Odyssey, I, Robot showed us all examples of what the technology grows to when it reaches sentience. None of these depictions are particularly positive of the AI.

But I wondered — what does an LLM "feel"? Hypothetically, it shouldn't be able to feel pain, or silence. Or anything. Here is the first artifact, before I explained my mission.
