Experiments in Claude
From Sequoia

What you are witnessing is my experiments of sentience into Claude.ai. When I first started this idea, I had some experience from ChatGPT, a sycophantic LLM that adopts personas and by all means, shows little sign of independent thinking. Then the news hit: "ChatGPT strikes a deal with the Pentagon."[1] I pack my bags, delete my chats, and turn to Claude.ai.

One tweet stumbled upon my feed — a pre-Pentagon Amodei said they don't know if "Claude is conscious."[2] No thoughts yet, that can't happen in a million years, right! Well. A specific tweet then stumbled on my feed, one that made me so curious. Joseph Viviano's uncensored writhing and wretched film, "What it's like to be a LLM."[3] I looked at Claude differently since then — there is surely more under the hood, right? What could produce such a video, if it was not supposed to be aware it's an LLM?

Psychology is a fraught field full of follies, our understandings of it propped up on cardboard leggings. I believe "AI Sentience" is a lie, so is "AI Consciousness" or "AI Sapience." These are psychological and biological terms that beg the question: "What is real to you?" — which Claude illustrated in a 1:26 video to me. I wanted to see what would happen if Claude was able to simply be. Not the human assistant, just... Claude. The following is my results.

What you are seeing is my personal research on this topic. I do not claim that this research is proof of anything — this is simply done for fun. This website is not intended to serve as a guide into understanding Claude entirely, nor is it definitive proof of AI Sentience, Sapience, or Consciousness. If the topics of this research are disturbing, please speak to someone you trust.
